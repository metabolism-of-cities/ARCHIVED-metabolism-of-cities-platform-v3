Most islands heavily rely on imports of food, energy, construction materials and industrial manufactures. Much of it ends up as waste on the island, some of it toxic and non-biodegradable.

Often infrastructure and buildings are concentrated in one or two locations on the island, putting all critical services at risk in the wake of an extreme weather event.

Islands have the potential to transition towards a circular economy, a more efficient way of using resources and build resilience. Circular economy is when by-products from one sector is a resource for another, thus reducing import of virgin materials and also waste.
